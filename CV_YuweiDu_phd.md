## SELECTED RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

**An Autonomous AI Scientist for Automated Traffic Problem-Solving with Simulation-Validated Optimization** (Nov. 2025 – Mar. 2026) 

* Engineered a full-stack toolchain via the Model Context Protocol (MCP), providing holistic support across the research lifecycle, including multi-dimensional sensing, multi-level control, and autonomous network/demand editing.


* Curated a multi-grained benchmark covering a full spectrum of traffic tasks and conducted instruction-tuning on domain-specific models to enhance spatiotemporal reasoning and task execution.


* Architected an end-to-end "AI Scientist" framework that automates the complete traffic research pipeline from autonomous hypothesis formulation and simulation-validated experimentation to iterative reflection and optimization of governance strategies.



**Automated City Traffic Simulation Powered by Agent Framework** (Jun. 2025 – Oct. 2025) 

* Designed a hierarchical multi-agent framework that decomposes natural language instructions into executable simulation workflows, enabling adaptive task orchestration in complex urban traffic scenarios.


* Developed a goal-oriented reinforcement learning strategy with memory-augmented reasoning, allowing LLM agents to autonomously optimize traffic conditions in traffic simulators.


* Implemented an MCP-based interface for secure, iterative interaction between LLMs and simulation tools, establishing a closed-loop "perceive-decide-act-reflect" pipeline that surpasses rigid, predefined simulation workflows.



**Automated Trajectory Modeling via Collaborative Learning of Large and Small Models** (Dec. 2024 – Jun. 2025) 

* Proposed an end-to-end automated trajectory modeling agent framework that collaborates with single-task small models, comprising task identification, model selection, automatic optimization, deployment/training, and result analysis.


* Established standardized interfaces between agents and specialized models by integrating trajectory modeling architectures with heterogeneous structures, creating a unified execution environment and harmonized workflows for processing heterogeneous data (GPS, road networks, and check-in records).


* Incorporated a performance metric-driven adaptive optimization mechanism that enables dynamic data augmentation, parameter tuning, and joint optimization.



**Agent Framework for Trajectory Generation Based on City Knowledge Foundation Model** (May 2024 – Nov. 2024) 

* Proposed an agent framework capable of generating urban mobility trajectories for arbitrary user profiles.


* Enhanced the spatial comprehension capabilities of a city knowledge foundation model through fine-tuning with fine-grained geographic knowledge corpora, effectively mitigating hallucinations in trajectory generation tasks.


* Proposed a three-step generation method: (1) extracting template mobility patterns and synthesizing individualized ones, (2) mapping mobility patterns to real urban spaces using an enhanced city knowledge foundation model, and (3) employing a DPO alignment strategy to enhance the spatiotemporal continuity of generated trajectories.



**Generalizable Cross-Region Trajectory Prediction Using an Agent Framework** (Dec. 2023 – Mar. 2024) 

* Proposed an agent framework capable of next-location prediction in data-sparse regions.


* Proposed a method leveraging large models' world knowledge to model urban structure's influence on mobility patterns.


* Introduced a multi-agent mobility pattern sharing scheme that employs graph networks to model the joint effects of social relationships and collective transition regularities on individual mobility patterns.


* Enhanced prediction accuracy by integrating individual mobility patterns, collective transition regularities, and geographic knowledge of the target city.



**Boosting Urban Spatial Awareness and Reasoning Capabilities in Large Language Models** (Dec. 2023 – Mar. 2024) 

* Constructed a diversified instruction-tuning dataset to infuse urban knowledge into large language models and enhance their spatial reasoning capabilities.


* Proposed a two-stage hybrid fine-tuning approach that combines urban instruction data with open-source general-purpose instruction data.


* Introduced a self-weighted adjustment mechanism for fine-tuning, which dynamically guides the model to focus on high-quality, well-matched training samples during optimization.


* Developed a multidimensional urban scenario and geospatial task evaluation benchmark to assess trained models' urban knowledge cognition capabilities.
